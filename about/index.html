Here's my opinion on the last book I finished:

Dreams from My Father

An autobiography by Obama (before he even became a state senator), this is really a generational chronicle, narrating his upbringing, and perspective with that of his fathers, and even grandfather.

Initial Attraction
In one sentence, this Video. Due to not quite being in the public eye at this point, Obama's words are quite raw and honest, detailing everything from resentment, anger, and nihilism to drug use and trauma.

Overall
A great read, although a little boring during his community organizational work. Still his reflections on being an outsider in two-hybrid cultures, his resentment towards his nation, his maternal family, the establishment they represent, and his genuine search for some sense of heritage, are all universal themes that unfortunately remain timeless.

My Favourite Part
During his trip to Kenya, his meeting with his paternal family, and their narration of his grandfather's and his own father's lives. The contrast between the two, their differing views on the realities of a colonized and globalized world, are a great illustration of two generations' varying approaches and opportunities in a new time.

I love all things History! from the start of human civilization to contemporary geopolitics, History's intersection with creativity and storytelling, and of course, its' relevance to modernity, fascinate me.

Mark Twain's observation"History doesn't repeat itself but it often rhymes" rings true to me, the almost eerie relatability of our ancestors- despite all the "progress" dividing us- is quite astonishing.

Periods I have explored in the past include Antiquity, The Islamic Golden Age, Medieval Eurasia, The Age Of Discovery, The Gunpowder Empires, The Age of Imperialism, and Decolonization.

Currently, I'm preoccupied with the early modern period, the beginnings of true globalization in this period, the fundamental foundations of the modern world laid out (particularly regarding World-systems theory), and the infancy of current archetypes.

In the next few years, I hope to tackle the Victorian age, a much more recognizable world, which's consequences are much more easily translatable to current events.
